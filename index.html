Build Request

Build.

Fill in this brief so we can scope, design, and build your system. The more detail you provide, the better the final tool will be.

Seven questions  |  Takes 10-15 minutes  |  Exports to markdown
01 — The Vision

What do you want to build?

Describe the system, tool, or workflow you need. What does it do? Who interacts with it? Keep it plain — technical details aren't needed at this stage.

Examples: "A system that monitors competitor pricing daily and alerts me when something changes." "A workflow that takes a client brief and produces a full research pack in 20 minutes." "An agent that drafts personalised outreach for every new lead in our CRM."

Be as specific or as broad as you like — we'll refine it together.
02 — The Problem

What problem does this solve?

What's the pain point today? What takes too long, gets forgotten, or doesn't happen at all because nobody has time?

Specific is useful. "We spend two hours every Monday compiling the same report" is more actionable than "reporting could be better."

Think about what triggers the frustration — what happens (or doesn't) that made you think of this.
03 — The Audience

Who is this for?

Who will use this day-to-day? Just you, your team, a specific role, or the whole department? This determines complexity, output format, and how much context the system needs.

Also note who benefits indirectly — a system you use might produce output that goes to clients, leadership, or other teams.

Names, roles, or team descriptions all work here.
04 — Success

What does done look like?

How will you know it's working? Be concrete — "I open Slack on Monday morning and there's a briefing waiting" is more useful than "it saves time."

If there's a specific metric (time saved, volume produced, errors avoided), include it. Otherwise, describe the observable outcome.

The more concrete, the better we can design to hit that target.
05 — Inputs & Outputs

What goes in, what comes out?

What data or information does the system need? Does it pull from a specific tool, spreadsheet, CRM, or data source? Does someone feed it information, or should it find its own?

Then the output: a report, email draft, Slack message, dashboard, file? How often — daily, weekly, on-demand? What format works for the people who'll use it?

Include any specific tools, platforms, or data sources if you know them.
06 — Constraints

What are the boundaries?

Naming constraints early saves time. Deadlines, required tools, sensitive data, existing systems it needs to integrate with, budget for external APIs.

Also flag anything organisational — approvals needed, stakeholders who need to sign off, or processes that can't be changed.

No constraint is too small to mention — it's easier to design around them from the start.
07 — Your Steer

What should we emphasise or avoid?

Is there something you definitely want ("it must be simple to use") or definitely don't want ("no dashboards — nobody looks at them")? Have you seen something similar that worked, or something that failed?

Preferences and hunches are useful here. "The output should read like a colleague's summary, not a system-generated report" — that kind of steer shapes the build.

Hunches count. If something feels important, write it down.
